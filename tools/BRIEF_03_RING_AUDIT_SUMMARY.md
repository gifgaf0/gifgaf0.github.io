## Headline findings

### Algebraic structure of R_3329 (Task 1)

```
R_3329 = Z_3329[X] / (X^256 + 1)
       ≅ ⨁_{j=0}^{127} F_3329[X] / (X^2 - ζ^{2j+1}),    ζ = 17
       ≅ (F_{3329²})^{128}      (since 17 is a quadratic non-residue mod 3329)
```

Confirmed by: zeta has order 256 in F_3329×; product of the 128
quadratics reconstructs `X^256 + 1` exactly (mod 3329); each quadratic
is irreducible because every odd power of a non-residue is itself a
non-residue.

### The brief's Z₇ premise is false (Task 2)

The brief states *"3329 ≡ 1 (mod 7), so F_3329× contains a Z₇
subgroup."* Neither half is correct over F_3329:

- `3329 mod 7 = 4`, not 1.
- The relevant condition is `7 | (q − 1)`. Here `q − 1 = 3328 = 2⁸·13`,
  which has no factor of 7.

Therefore F_3329× contains no element of order 7, hence no Z₇
subgroup. The Singer-cycle orbit construction described in
`sedenion_Fp.py` cannot run with this prime as input. The orbit
identity `128 = 7·18 + 2` is arithmetic only; it is not a theorem
about an actual Z₇ orbit on this factor set.

The natural cyclic action that *does* exist is Z/128, generated by
multiplication by `ζ²`, with prime factorisation `2⁷`.

### ZD pair graph (Task 3)

Because each CRT factor is a field (F_{3329²}), all zero divisors of
R_3329 come from the product structure. Every pair `{i, j}` of
factors supports a zero-divisor pair (orthogonal idempotents
multiply to zero). Hence

```
G_ZD = K_128,   |E| = 8128,   Aut(G_ZD) = Sym(128).
```

The PSL(2,7) / K_{7,7} sub-question is moot in two ways:

1. There is no Z₇ subgroup of F_3329× to lift to PSL(2,7).
2. Even if there were, K_{7,7} is automatically a subgraph of any
   14-vertex induced subgraph of K_128 (since K_14 ⊃ K_{7,7}); so
   "produces a K_{7,7}-like subgraph" in this graph carries no
   algebraic content.

The natural ring-induced subgroup of `Aut(G_ZD)` is the dihedral
`D_128` of order 256 (rotation by `ζ²` and the inversion `X ↦ X⁻¹`).

### (2,3,7) angle triples (Task 4)

Of all `C(128, 3) = 341 376` unordered triples of primitive 256-th
roots of unity in F_3329, the number whose discrete logs are exactly
proportional to `21 : 14 : 6` (mod 256, up to scalar and permutation)
is **0**.

Reason (raw observation): the proportion components 14 and 6 are
both even, so `14m mod 256` and `6m mod 256` are always even —
incompatible with primitive 256-th roots, which sit at odd indices.

Lifting the restriction to the full 256-element circle, the count is
**248** unordered triples, equal under the brief's definition to the
expected count under a uniform random null. So the full-circle count
matches the uniform-random null by construction; no excess.
