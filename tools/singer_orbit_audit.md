# Singer-Cycle / Z₇ Orbit Audit on the 128 NTT Factors of R_3329

## Premise check — and what the check finds

The brief states: *"3329 ≡ 1 (mod 7), so F_3329× contains a Z₇ subgroup."*
Both clauses of that sentence are false over F_3329:

```
3329 mod 7   = 4          (not 1)
(q − 1) = 3328 = 2^8 · 13
3328 mod 7   = 3          (so 7 does NOT divide q − 1)
```

The condition that determines whether F_q× contains a Z₇ subgroup is
whether **7 divides q − 1**, not whether q ≡ 1 (mod 7). Here

```
|F_3329×| = 3328 = 2^8 · 13
```

contains no factor of 7, so by Lagrange there is **no element of order
7 in F_3329×**, and therefore no nontrivial Z₇ subgroup.

Per the brief: *"Do not interpret results. Write the raw findings."*
That is the raw finding for this prime.

## Consequences for the requested audit

The Singer-cycle orbit construction in `sedenion_Fp.py` (as described
to me) starts by picking a generator g of a Z₇ subgroup and pushing it
through the A-matrix action on factor indices. With no Z₇ subgroup
available in F_3329×, that procedure has no input.

Three things follow directly:

1. **No nontrivial Z₇ orbit decomposition of the 128 factors exists**
   that comes from a Z₇ ⊂ F_3329×. The only Z₇ action on the 128-set
   is by external relabelling (i.e., choose an arbitrary embedding
   Z₇ ↪ Sym(128)); that's not what `sedenion_Fp.py` is doing.

2. **The orbit-size prediction `128 = 7·18 + 2` is vacuous** for
   F_3329. The arithmetic identity holds, but it is not a statement
   about a Z₇ orbit on this factor set, because there is no such
   action coming from the field structure.

3. **The closest natural cyclic subgroup acting on the 128 factors**
   is the Z/128 generated by `zeta^2` (rotation of the index `j` by
   1 in `r_j = zeta^(2j+1)`). Its order is 128, with prime
   factorisation 2^7. There is no order-7 element anywhere inside it.

## What CAN be enumerated

For completeness, the prime divisors of the multiplicative-group order
of F_3329× are 2 and 13. The corresponding subgroup orders that
*do* exist as cyclic subgroups of F_3329× are:

```
|H| ∈ { 1, 2, 4, 8, 13, 16, 26, 32, 52, 64, 104, 128, 208, 256, 416, 832, 1664, 3328 }
```

(every divisor of 2^8 · 13). Of these, the ones that act non-trivially
on the 128 NTT factor indices via multiplication-by-zeta on the root
side (i.e., `r → η · r` where `η ∈ H ∩ ⟨zeta⟩`) are those H whose
intersection with ⟨zeta⟩ is non-trivial. ⟨zeta⟩ has order 256, so this
intersection equals H whenever |H| | 256, i.e. for |H| ∈ {2, 4, 8, 16,
32, 64, 128, 256}.

Each of those gives the **expected** orbit structure on the 128 odd
residues mod 256 (= the index `k = 2j+1`):

| |H| | # orbits | orbit size | comment |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2 | 64 | 2 | swaps `k ↔ k+128` (≡ multiplication by `zeta^128 = -1`) |
| 4 | 32 | 4 | rotation by `zeta^64`; `zeta^64 = i` (sqrt(-1)) |
| 8 | 16 | 8 | rotation by `zeta^32` |
| 16 | 8 | 16 | rotation by `zeta^16` |
| 32 | 4 | 32 | rotation by `zeta^8` |
| 64 | 2 | 64 | rotation by `zeta^4` |
| 128 | 1 | 128 | the natural cyclic orbit; rotation by `zeta^2` |
| 256 | 1 | 128 | same orbit (already transitive at order 128) |

There is **no row for |H| = 7** because `7 ∤ |F_q×|`.

If the master document expects a Z₇ Singer cycle, that expectation is
calibrated to a different prime than 3329, presumably one with `q ≡ 1
(mod 7)` and with `7 | q − 1`. Off-the-shelf candidates that *would*
admit a Z₇ subgroup include 29, 43, 71, 113, 127, 197, 211, 239, ...,
but none of those is ML-KEM's prime.

## Conclusion (raw finding only)

| Item | Result |
|---|---|
| Does F_3329× contain a Z₇ subgroup? | **No.** `7 ∤ 3328`. |
| Singer-cycle orbit structure on 128 factors via Z₇? | **Undefined** — premise fails. |
| Orbit-size identity `128 = 7·18 + 2` agreement | Arithmetic only; not a theorem about this ring. |
| Natural cyclic subgroup on the 128 factors | Z/128, generated by multiplication by `zeta^2`. |

Outputs of the cyclic-subgroup audit are reproducible from
`tools/_ring_factors.py` and the table above.
